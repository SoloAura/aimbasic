// 01 — Fundamentals

THE PILLARS

Everything in Valorant aim traces back to four non-negotiable pillars. Master these and the rank will follow.

Critical

Crosshair Placement

01

The single highest-leverage habit in Valorant. Keep your crosshair at head height at all times — not the floor, not the walls, not the air. Pre-aim every corner at the exact pixel where an enemy head will appear.

Impact on Win Rate 92%
Difficulty to Learn 35%
Pro Tip

On Haven A-Long, your crosshair should already be at head height near the box before you even see an enemy. Time-to-kill drops by ~60ms when you don't have to adjust.

Mechanical

Counter-Strafing

02

You cannot reliably hit shots while moving. Counter-strafing means pressing the opposite key (A while moving D, or vice versa) to stop your character instantly, allowing accurate shots.

Impact on Accuracy 88%
Time to Master 70%

Weapon

Spray Control

03

Every rifle in Valorant has a fixed spray pattern. The Vandal and Phantom both kick up and drift. Learn to pull down and compensate horizontally — first 5 bullets are the window for headshots.

Vandal
• High recoil, consistent pattern
• One-tap potential at any range
• Reset every 4-5 bullets
• Pull-down: ~8° over 15 shots
Phantom
• Lower recoil, faster fire rate
• Forgiving at close-mid range
• Suppressed — no tracers
• No one-tap beyond 15m
💡 Start tapping and bursting (2-3 shots) at medium-long range. Full-spraying past 10m on Vandal loses you the duel.
Movement

Peek Mechanics

04

How you peek a corner determines who wins the duel before you even see each other. Wide-peeking, shoulder-peeking, and jiggle-peeking all have specific use cases.

Wide Peek
Gain angle advantage. Move far from the corner to see them before they can track to you. Best vs. stationary defenders.
Shoulder Peek
Flash your shoulder to bait the shot, then trade. Used to gather info and waste enemy bullets without committing.
Jiggle Peek
Spam A+D micro-movements to be unpredictable. Confuses operators and players who pre-aim. High-risk in open lanes.

// 06 — Mindset

THE MENTAL GAME

Mechanics ceiling. Mentality floor. You will never consistently rank up if your mental isn't right — even with perfect aim.

01

VOD Review Every Loss

Watch your worst round of every loss. Identify: where was your crosshair when you died? Were you moving? Did you have info? One insight per game compounds to 300 insights per year.

02

Kill the Tilt Loop

Tilt → bad decisions → more deaths → more tilt. Break it. 5-minute rule: if you die stupidly twice in a row, stand up and walk away from your desk. Return only when you're calm.

03

Focus on Process, Not Rank

Set session goals like "proper crosshair placement" — not "win 3 games." Rank is a lagging indicator. If you focus on process, rank follows. Focusing on rank directly causes panic and bad decisions.

04

Never Blame Teammates

You control one player. Blaming others is cognitive offloading — your brain uses it to avoid self-examination. Ask: "What could I have done differently?" Every round has an answer.

05

Session Limits

Stop at 2 consecutive losses. Forced break. Your brain is in a negative state and it affects aim, decision-making, and communication. More games don't fix a losing mental loop — they entrench it.

06

Sleep & Hydration

This isn't a joke. Reaction time drops 10–15% when dehydrated and 20%+ with poor sleep. Peak Valorant requires your nervous system functioning at its best. Treat it like any sport.

// 07 — Maps

MAP KNOWLEDGE WINS DUELS

You don't need better aim to win — you need to take better duels. Map knowledge tells you which duels to take.

Ascent
Symmetric

Wide open mid with crucial cat control. Mid fights are long-range — Vandal preferred. A-main fights are aggressive peek corridors.

🎯 Pre-aim: A-lobby boxes at head height
🎯 Hold mid doors with crossed angles
🎯 B-site: Clear market before pushing
Haven
3 Sites

Three bomb sites = attackers can spread defenders thin. C-long is a sniper's paradise. A-long favors aggressive peekers.

🎯 C-long: Pre-aim corner, then room
🎯 A-long: Wide-peek to gain angle advantage
🎯 Mid: Shoulder peek garage door first
Bind
Teleporters

No mid. Teleporters enable unexpected angles. Short A fights and tight B site hallways — spray control over flicking.

🎯 A short: Hug wall, wide-peek hookah
🎯 B site: Pre-aim lamps before entering
🎯 Teleporter: Always check the sound first
Split
Vertical

Tight corridors and ramps. Vertical play is key — learn to check heights. Phantom shines on Split due to close-range fights.

🎯 Mid: Check vents before walking under
🎯 A ramps: Peek wide to cut angles
🎯 B main: Shoulder peek showers first
Icebox
Complex

Elevated positions and container stacks. Always check high ground. Sniper-friendly B-tube. Use grenades to clear boxes before pushing.

🎯 Mid: Check yellow box and nest
🎯 A: Pre-aim the boxes at site entry
🎯 B tube: Operator-heavy — flash or rush
Sunset
Mid-Focus

Mid control is decisive. Market fight determines A-site access. Long sightlines at B-main reward precise Vandal taps.

🎯 Market: Never push without a flash
🎯 A-main: Wide peek the corner first
🎯 B: One-taps from mid are common
// 08 — Mistakes

STOP DOING THIS

✕
Shooting While Moving
In Valorant, movement and accuracy are mutually exclusive. Every shot you fire mid-strafe is a prayer, not a shot. Stop. Shoot. Move.
✕
Peeking Without Info
Blind aggressive peeks are gambling, not playing. Use abilities, callouts, or sound cues before peeking an unknown corner. Patience is mechanical skill.
✕
Spray and Pray at Long Range
Full spray beyond close range loses you every duel. Tap or 3-shot burst at medium range. Counter-strafe between each burst. Let the pattern reset.
✕
Force-Buying Every Round
A Spectre vs. full rifles is a massive disadvantage. Eco properly, spike team economy, and you'll win more rounds overall — even the ones you eco.
✕
Chasing Kills Over Objectives
Valorant rounds are won by planting and defusing, not by K/D. A 40-kill loss is still a loss. Every round-winning decision should center around the spike.
✕
Playing on High Ping
Above 60ms, peeker's advantage swings hard against you. Fix your connection: wired over WiFi, close background apps, select the right server. 20ms vs. 80ms is a mechanical difference.
